01The idea

A guard you actually own

Wardnet is a self-hosted privacy gateway — per-device VPN routing, DNS ad-blocking, and a web dashboard in a single binary. The brand has to feel trustworthy and technical, but also a little defiant: this is infrastructure you control, not a service that controls you.

Ward

To guard, to protect. The shield is the oldest, clearest symbol of defense — and it carries straight over from the existing mark.

net

Your network. The W inside is built from five linked nodes — devices wired into one protected fabric, not bolted on after.

Self-hosted

It runs on your Raspberry Pi, your rules. The voice and visuals stay plainspoken and developer-first, never corporate.

02Logo

04Usage

Backgrounds & misuse

Keep contrast high and the mark untouched. When in doubt, white or ink wordmark with an emerald mark is always safe.

On ink → emerald
On paper → ink
On emerald → ink
On white → emerald

DO

✓Use the supplied vector files at their native proportions.
✓Keep the mark a single solid color from the palette.
✓Give it the full clear space, even in tight layouts.
✓Drop to the solid-shield favicon below 32 px.

DON'T

✕Stretch, rotate, or add shadows and gradients to the mark.
✕Recolor it with off-palette or multiple colors.
✕Re-typeset the wordmark in any font other than Archivo.
✕Place it on busy imagery or low-contrast backgrounds.

07Voice & tone

How Wardnet talks

Like a sharp engineer explaining something to a peer: plain, precise, and quietly confident. We respect that the user is in control — because, literally, they are.

01

Plainspoken

Say what it does in words a person uses out loud. No jargon for jargon's sake.

02

Confident, never hype

State facts, skip superlatives. The product earns trust; the copy doesn't oversell.

03

Hand over control

It's their hardware, their data, their rules. We guide; we never gatekeep.

04

Precise where it counts

For setup, config, and CLI, be exact and technical. Developers are the audience.

We write

"Block ads and trackers for every device on your network."

"Runs on a Raspberry Pi, a mini-PC, or any Linux host."

"Set it up in minutes with a single command."

"Your network. Your rules."

Not this

"Leverage best-in-class DNS-layer threat mitigation."

"Deployable across heterogeneous edge-compute substrates."

"A frictionless, rapid time-to-value onboarding journey."

"Empowering synergistic network governance."

09Files

Asset library

SVG is the source of truth for every asset. Use the outlined files from assets/dist/ - they are font-independent and render identically everywhere.

SVGwardnet-icon.svgApp icon · squircle + mark
SVGwardnet-mark-emerald · ink · white.svgMark only · 3 colors
SVGwardnet-logo-light · dark.svgHorizontal lockup
SVGwardnet-logo-tagline-light · dark.svgLockup + tagline

Fonts are free & open-source: Archivo, Hanken Grotesk, and JetBrains Mono (all on Google Fonts, OFL).

Generating PNGs

PNGs are not committed — generate them at the exact size you need from the dist/ SVGs using the build-png.js tool in assets/tools/.

# from assets/tools/ — install deps once
yarn install
# node build-png.js <input.svg> <width> [output.png]
node build-png.js ../dist/wardnet-icon.svg 512
node build-png.js ../dist/wardnet-icon.svg 192 icon-192.png
node build-png.js ../dist/wardnet-logo-light.svg 2048
node build-png.js ../dist/wardnet-mark-emerald.svg 32 favicon-32.png

Output defaults to the same directory as the SVG with a -<width> suffix (e.g. wardnet-icon-512.png). Always use the outlined files from dist/ — the src/ files contain live text that may not render correctly without the fonts installed.
